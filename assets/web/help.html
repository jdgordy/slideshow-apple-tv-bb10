Overview

Slideshow for Apple TV allows you to stream a slideshow of pictures from your BlackBerry 10 device to your television set equipped with an Apple TV (2nd- or 3rd-generation) set-top box.

Apple TV Setup

  1. Turn on your Apple TV and connect it to your WiFi network.
  2. To enable AirPlay: choose Settings / AirPlay and turn AirPlay ON.
  3. Ensure that Onscreen Code is turned OFF.
  4. To password-protect your Apple TV (optional): choose Settings / AirPlay / Set Password and specify a password.

BlackBerry 10 Setup

  1. Connect to your WiFi network and launch Slideshow for Apple TV.
  2. Choose Devices to begin AirPlay device discovery and select your Apple TV from the list of available devices.
  3. If your Apple TV is password-protected, the application will prompt you to enter the password.
  4. Select and organize pictures for your slideshow:
    • Choose Pictures and select a list of pictures stored on your device or media card, or
    • Launch the Pictures or File Manager application, and invoke the Share action to send one or more pictures to Slideshow for Apple TV.
  5. To begin the slideshow: choose Start and the application will connect to your Apple TV.
  6. Managing the slideshow:
    • Choose Previous or Next to move through the list of pictures.
    • Choose Stop to end the slideshow and disconnect from your Apple TV.

Optional Features

To tune and improve slideshow performance for your own WiFi network, choose Settings from the application menu and adjust the options listed below:

  • Connection Timeout - Sets the network response timeout for picture transfers between your BlackBerry and Apple TV devices.
  • Scale Oversize Images - Enables dynamic scaling of pictures larger than your TV screen dimensions to reduce network transfer (caching) time. Try enabling this option if your slideshow contains large pictures and you experience long picture caching times during the slideshow.
  • Screen Size - Specifies your TV screen size in vertical pixels. If Scale Oversize Images is enabled, set this to match your TV dimensions.
  • Image Quality - Sets the JPEG quality setting used to dynamically scale images. If Scale Oversize Images is enabled, set this to low to reduce quality and speed up picture caching, or high to improve picture quality at the expense of picture caching speed.
